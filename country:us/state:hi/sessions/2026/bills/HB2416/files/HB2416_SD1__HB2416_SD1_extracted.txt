Section 1:
HB2416 SD1 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.B. NO. 2416 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 H.D. 1 STATE OF HAWAII S.D. 1 A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO A MARITIME YOUTH APPRENTICESHIP PATHWAY PROGRAM. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that Hawaii's maritime sector, including shipbuilding and repair, port and harbor operations, marine transportation, and ocean-based industries, is essential to the State's economic resilience, supply chain reliability, and emergency preparedness. The legislature further finds that Hawaii faces persistent workforce shortages in maritime occupations due to an aging workforce, limited training pipelines, and insufficient exposure of young people to viable, high-wage career pathways. Many young adults lack access to hands-on, paid training opportunities that lead directly to family-sustaining employment, contributing to youth out-migration and the loss of local talent. The legislature recognizes that maritime careers provide strong earning potential, transferable technical skills, and meaningful work closely connected to Hawaii's island geography and ocean-based economy.  Expanding structured apprenticeship and pre-apprenticeship pathways for individuals ages sixteen to twenty-four will create new opportunities for local youth to remain in Hawaii while entering skilled, in-demand professions. The legislature further finds the university of Hawaii community college system has demonstrated expertise in technical education, workforce training, and apprenticeship-aligned instruction.  The community college system is well positioned to build upon the current momentum of the existing youth pre‑apprenticeship work of Windward community college and Kailua high school and lead a comprehensive, statewide study on the development of a maritime youth apprenticeship pathway. The legislature also finds that in accordance with article X, section 6, of the Hawaii State Constitution, this Act involves a matter of statewide concern. Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to develop apprenticeship opportunities in maritime occupations by: (1)  Requiring the university of Hawaii community college system to conduct a study and develop a plan for a state-sponsored maritime youth apprenticeship pathway program; (2)  Establishing clear accountability and reporting requirements for the study; and (3)  Appropriating funds to support this work, without prematurely establishing or mandating a permanent program structure. SECTION 2. (a)  The university of Hawaii community college system, together with Honolulu community college and Windward community college, shall conduct a comprehensive study and develop a plan for a state-sponsored maritime youth apprenticeship pathway program serving individuals sixteen to twenty-four years of age. (b) The university of Hawaii community college system shall be provided with the necessary workforce and enrollment data to support the study's findings from: (1)  The department of business, economic development, and tourism; (2)  The department of education; and (3)  The department of labor and industrial relations. The university of Hawaii community college system shall also request the participation of maritime industry employers and labor representatives, including shipyards, port and harbor operators, marine transportation companies, and marine service providers. (c) The study and apprenticeship plan shall include: (1)  A list of curriculum and instructional requirements that align with secondary and postsecondary career and technical education opportunities; (2)  An assessment of how the program will meet state and federal apprenticeship standards and provide industry‑recognized credentials; (3)  An outline of the pathway from high school coursework and pre-apprenticeship into postsecondary training and registered apprenticeships; (4)  An assessment of employer engagement strategies, including paid on-the-job training opportunities, mentorships, and work-based learning models; (5)  Any resource requirements, including faculty and staff, facilities, equipment, and administrative support; (6)  Any funding considerations and potential public‑private partnership models; and (7)  Implementation options, including discussions of pilot phases, scalability, and geographic reach. SECTION 3. The university of Hawaii community college system shall submit a report of its findings and recommendations, including a proposed program structure, estimated costs, implementation options, and any proposed legislation, to the legislature no later than twenty days prior to the convening of the regular session of 2027. SECTION 4.  There is appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii the sum of $           or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 to conduct a study and develop a plan for a maritime youth apprenticeship pathway program, including faculty and staff needs, research and analysis, stakeholder engagement, curriculum and pathway design, and reporting requirements. The sum appropriated shall be expended by the university of Hawaii for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 5. There is appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii the sum of $           or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 to cover the costs of a temporary project coordinator, data consultants, and statewide industry workshops. The sum appropriated shall be expended by the university of Hawaii for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 6. This Act shall take effect on July 31, 2055. Report Title: UH; Maritime Youth Apprenticeship Pathway Program; Study; University of Hawaii Community College System; Workforce Development; Report; Appropriation Description: Directs the University of Hawaii Community College System, in partnership with state agencies and maritime employers, to conduct a study and develop a plan for a statewide Maritime Youth Apprenticeship Pathway Program for individuals ages 16 to 24.  Requires a report to the Legislature with recommendations on program structure, resource needs, proposed legislation and implementation options.  Appropriates funds.  Effective 7/31/2055.  (SD1) The summary description of legislation appearing on this page is for informational purposes only and is not legislation or evidence of legislative intent.
