Section 1:
HB2345 SD2 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.B. NO. 2345 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 H.D. 1 STATE OF HAWAII S.D. 2 A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO SCHOOL FACILITIES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that the centralized system for managing school capital improvement projects within the department of education has contributed to a substantial backlog of these projects.  This structure has resulted in delays, reduced responsiveness to the needs of individual school communities, and challenges in managing project timelines and budgets. The legislature further finds that the school facilities authority was established by Act 72, Session Laws of Hawaii 2020 (Act 72), as a specialized agency focused exclusively on the development and improvement of public school facilities.  Act 72 intended the consolidation of facilities-related expertise within the authority, including the transfer of certain functions and positions from the department of education.  That consolidation has not yet been implemented. The legislature also finds that a decentralized, district‑based model for managing capital improvement projects will improve efficiency, accountability, and responsiveness.  Localizing management within geographically based districts will allow decisions to be made closer to the schools they affect and provide a phased framework for the authority to assume broader capital improvement responsibilities in the future. The purpose of this Act is to modernize the State's school facilities management by: (1)  Requiring the school facilities authority to establish geographically based capital improvement project districts for the localized management of capital improvement projects; (2)  Transferring vacant facilities development branch positions from the department of education to the school facilities authority; and (3)  Transferring all functions, duties, and funds related to capital improvement projects from the department of education to the school facilities authority. SECTION 2.  Chapter 302A, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding two new sections to part VI, subpart C, to be appropriately designated and to read as follows: "§302A-     Capital improvement project districts; established.  (a)  The authority shall establish geographically based capital improvement project districts to provide for the localized management of project design, construction, and administration related to capital improvement projects.  The boundaries of these districts shall, to the extent practicable, align with the school districts established for administrative purposes by the department. (b)  Each geographically based capital improvement project district shall be responsible for: (1)  Providing localized management and oversight of capital improvement project design and construction; (2)  Administering project budgets and managing project schedules to ensure timely and cost-effective completion; and (3)  Serving as the primary local point of contact for public schools within the district regarding capital improvement projects and related needs. (c)  The authority shall assign dedicated staff, including a district facilities manager, to each geographically based capital improvement project district to carry out the functions described in this section. §302A-     Use of general funds for operations and management; prohibited.  General funds shall not be expended for the operations or management of the authority." SECTION 3.  (a) All rights, powers, functions, and duties of the department of education relating to capital improvement projects are transferred to the school facilities authority. (b)  All vacant positions within the department of education's facilities development branch as of the effective date of this Act, including all associated funding for salaries and benefits, are transferred to the school facilities authority. (c)  All appropriations, items of appropriation, and unexpended balances of funds, including any federal funds, designated for capital improvement projects within the department of education are transferred to the school facilities authority. SECTION 4.  There is appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii the sum of $           or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 for the purposes of establishing geographically based capital improvement project districts and implementing the transfer of positions and funds pursuant to this Act. The sum appropriated shall be expended by the school facilities authority for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 5.  New statutory material is underscored. SECTION 6.  This Act shall take effect on July 31, 2055. Report Title: DOE; SFA; CIP; Districts; Transfer; Appropriation Description: Requires the School Facilities Authority to establish geographically based capital improvement project districts for the localized management of capital improvement projects. Transfers all capital improvement project functions, funds, and vacant Facilities Development Branch positions from the Department of Education to the School Facilities Authority.  Clarifies that general funds shall not be used for operations or management of the Authority.  Appropriates funds.  Effective 7/31/2055.  (SD2) The summary description of legislation appearing on this page is for informational purposes only and is not legislation or evidence of legislative intent.
