A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO AGRICULTURE.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that increasing local
2 agricultural production is essential to the long-term
3 sustainability and food security of the State. However, local
4 agricultural producers face significant economic challenges,
5 particularly with the high costs of transporting agricultural
6 products and necessary supplies across the State. In a recent
7 survey report on Hawaii commercial agricultural expansion,
8 thirty-nine per cent of participating local ranchers and farmers
9 cited shipping and transportation costs as a leading barrier to
10 the expansion of their agricultural production. These
11 transportation costs place undue strain and financial burdens on
12 farmers and ranchers, limiting their ability to compete in local
13 markets and reducing the overall productivity of Hawaii's
14 agricultural sector. Additionally, in response to the economic
15 tariffs being implemented by the Trump administration, the
16 department of transportation harbors division reported that
17 effective July 1, 2025, the tariff for wharfage and pipeline

2 to the strain on local producers.
3 The legislature recognizes that, to successfully scale up
4 local production, the establishment of a cost reimbursement
5 program that offsets the increased transportation costs of
6 agricultural producers is necessary to ensure growth in the
7 agricultural sector. The financial relief provided by a cost
8 reimbursement program would help strengthen’ local agriculture,
9 encourage greater self-sufficiency, support the State's broader
10 efforts to diversify its economy, and promote sustainable
11 agricultural practices.
12 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to establish a
13 local agriculture transportation cost reimbursement pilot
14 program to ensure that certain local agricultural producers,
15 including those that manage livestock and produce essential
16 commodities and those engaged in floriculture and nursery
17 production, receive support to offset the high costs of
18 transporting goods to market and to obtain necessary supplies.
