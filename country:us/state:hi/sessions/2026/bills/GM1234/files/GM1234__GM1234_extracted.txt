1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that one in five Hawaii
2 residents has a disability, and many require functional or
3 access support before, during, and after disasters. These
4 individual's often encounter disproportionate risks, including
5 difficulty evacuating without proper supports; lack of
6 accessible transportation; barriers to receiving emergency
7 alerts and evacuation orders in formats that they can
8 understand; and obstacles in accessing shelters, emergency
9 medical care, and post-disaster relief programs, such as
10 housing, food, and financial assistance.
11 The legislature further finds that in the immediate
12 aftermath of the 2023, Maui wildfires, many individuals with
13 disabilities faced significant impediments to accessing
14 emergency shelters and services. Shelters often lacked physical
15 accessibility, disability-trained staff, and coordinated
16 resources to address medical and functional needs. Nonprofit
17 organizations and community members stepped in to fill these
