Section 1:
HB2039 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.B. NO. 2039 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 STATE OF HAWAII A BILL FOR AN ACT relating to capital improvement Projects for the benefit of the Thirty-second REPRESENTATIVE district. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The director of finance is authorized to issue general obligation bonds in the sum of $5,790,500 or so much thereof as may be necessary and the same sum or so much thereof as may be necessary is appropriated for fiscal year 2026-2027 for the purpose of capital improvement projects for the thirty-second representative district, as follows: 1.        Moanalua High School, Oahu Design, construction, and equipment and appurtenances for replacement of central air system for student center, building T, which currently has no open windows or other source of ventilation. Design                                 $250,000 Construction                           $1,000,000 Equipment                              $500,000 Total funding                     $1,750,000 2.        Radford High School, Oahu Plans, design, construction, and equipment and appurtenances for purchase and installation of a jumbotron score board for the athletic stadium. Total funding                          $500,000 3.        Moanalua Elementary School, Oahu Construction for completion of the Multi-Purpose Innovation Center Phase 2, which will add air conditioning and square footage. Total funding                          $2,900,000 4.        Salt Lake Elementary School, Oahu Plans, land, design, construction, and equipment and appurtenances for a covered basketball court with security fencing. Plans                                  $50,000 Land                                   $1,000 Design    `                            $9,000 Construction                           $150,000 Equipment                              $40,000 Total funding                     $250,000 5.        Aiea High School, Oahu A.   Plans, land, design, construction, and equipment and appurtenances for gymnasium exterior leveling and drainage. Total funding                     $192,500 B.   Plans, land, design, construction, and equipment and appurtenances to replace and update the football stadium perimeter fencing. Total funding                     $198,000 SECTION 2.  The appropriation made for the capital improvement projects authorized by this Act shall not lapse at the end of the fiscal year for which the appropriation is made; provided that all moneys from the appropriation that are unencumbered as of June 30, 2029, shall lapse as of that date. SECTION 3.  The sums appropriated for the respective capital projects set forth in section 1 shall be expended by the department of education for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 4.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.
