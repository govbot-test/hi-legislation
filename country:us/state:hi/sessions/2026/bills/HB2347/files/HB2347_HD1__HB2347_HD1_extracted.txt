Section 1:
HB2347 HD1 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.B. NO. 2347 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 H.D. 1 STATE OF HAWAII A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO VEHICLE SAFETY. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  (a)  The department of transportation may implement a pilot program for mobile vehicle safety inspections that allows mobile vehicle safety inspections for passenger cars to be conducted at any location determined by the vehicle owner and inspector; provided that each county shall have one participating operator who is authorized to conduct mobile vehicle safety inspections; provided further that for any county that consists of more than one island, there shall be one participating operator who is authorized to conduct mobile vehicle safety inspections per island. (b) The director of transportation may: (1)  Appoint up to three inspectors to support the pilot program; and (2)  Raise the price of the mobile vehicle safety inspections as part of the pilot program. (c)  The department of transportation may adopt rules necessary for the implementation of the pilot program. SECTION 2. This Act shall take effect on July 1, 3000; provided that: (1)  The pilot program established pursuant to section 1 of this Act shall commence one hundred twenty days after the effective date of this Act; and (2)  The pilot program shall be repealed on June 30, 2030. Report Title: Mobile Vehicle Safety Inspections; Pilot Program; Department of Transportation Description: Authorizes the Department of Transportation to implement a pilot program to allow for mobile vehicle safety inspections for passenger cars.  Establishes a four-year sunset date on the pilot program.  Effective 7/1/3000.  (HD1) The summary description of legislation appearing on this page is for informational purposes only and is not legislation or evidence of legislative intent.
