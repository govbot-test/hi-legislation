A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO TRAFFIC SAFETY.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 PART I
2 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that the State's roadways
3 may present safety risks to individuals that are stopped on the
4 shoulder or roadside due to a disabled vehicle. Although
5 existing law requires motorists to slow down and move over when
6 approaching certain emergency vehicles, the law allows unsafe
7 passing for all other vehicles. According to the National
8 Highway Traffic Safety Administration, nearly three thousand six
9 hundred fatalities occurred nationwide between 2014 and 2022
10 when ordinary drivers and passengers were stranded in or near
11 their disabled vehicles.
12 The legislature further finds that, as of 2025, twenty-nine
13 states have enacted comprehensive "move over" laws to cover all
14 disabled vehicles displaying hazard warning lights or other
15 recognized safety signals.

4 SECTION 2. Chapter 291C, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is
5 amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated
6 and to read as follows:
7 "§291C- Stationary vehicle stopped for emergencies;
8 duty of approaching vehicle. (a) A driver of a vehicle that is
9 approaching a stationary vehicle stopped in any location on the
10 roadway or shoulder, as Indicated by reasonable warning signals,
11 including vehicle hazard warning lights, road flares, traffic
12 cones, caution signs, or any other vehicular or nonvehicular
13 warning signs of the stopped stationary vehicle, shall slow down
14 to a reasonable and prudent speed that is safe under the
15 circumstances, and, if it is possible and safe to do so, make a
16 lane change to an adjacent lane; provided that if a lane change
17 is not possible or safe, the driver shall continue at a
18 reasonable and prudent speed and come to a complete stop if
19 necessary.
