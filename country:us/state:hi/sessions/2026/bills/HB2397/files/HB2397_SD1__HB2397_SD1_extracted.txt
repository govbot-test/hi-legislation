Section 1:
HB2397 SD1 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.B. NO. 2397 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 H.D. 2 STATE OF HAWAII S.D. 1 A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO SCHOOL FACILITIES. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that the State faces a critical shortage of early learning facilities and permanent space for public charter schools.  The downtown Honolulu area, in particular, has been identified as a high-need region for expanded pre-kindergarten capacity, yet available and appropriate space for new facilities remains limited.  The acquisition and renovation of an existing building represents a practical solution to expanding pre-kindergarten capacity in the downtown Honolulu area.  Because a building suitable for this purpose is likely to contain more space than required for a pre-kindergarten center alone, collocating a public charter school within the same facility allows for the efficient use of space and addresses multiple facility needs. The legislature further finds that the school impact fee program was established to ensure that new residential development contributes to the public infrastructure it necessitates.  However, school impact fees have largely remained unexpended.  The expansion of pre-kindergarten and public charter school capacity constitutes an appropriate and consistent use of these funds. Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to transfer school impact fees from the Kalihi-Ala Moana school impact district to the school impact fees subaccount of the school facilities special fund for the acquisition and renovation of one or more buildings, or the acquisition of land and construction of a new building, in the 96813 zip code to develop and expand pre-kindergarten and charter school capacity. SECTION 2.  Notwithstanding any other law to the contrary, there is transferred out from the school impact fees in EDN 400 for the Kalihi-Ala Moana school impact district the sum of $ to be deposited into the school impact fees subaccount of the school facilities special fund. SECTION 3.  There is appropriated out of the school impact fees subaccount of the school facilities special fund the sum of $           or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 to acquire and renovate one or more buildings, or acquire land and construct a new building, in the 96813 zip code to develop and expand pre-kindergarten and charter school capacity. The sum appropriated shall be expended by the school facilities authority for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 4.  The funds appropriated and expended under this Act shall be used to serve students residing in the Kalihi-Ala Moana school impact district.  Students residing outside the district may be enrolled only to the extent that space is available and in a manner consistent with the purpose of the fees under section 302A-1601, Hawaii Revised Statutes.  The expending agency shall administer these funds in accordance with applicable law, ensuring that in-district students are prioritized and that expenditures remain aligned with the impacts generated within the district. SECTION 5.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 3000. Report Title: Pre-Kindergarten; Charter Schools; Capacity; School Impact Fees; School Facilities Special Fund; Transfer; Appropriation Description: Transfers school impact fees from the Kalihi-Ala Moana school impact district to the School Impact Fees subaccount of the School Facilities Special Fund for the acquisition and renovation of one or more buildings, or the acquisition of land and construction of a new building, in the 96813 zip code to develop and expand pre-kindergarten and charter school capacity.  Provides that the funds appropriated and expended shall be used to serve students residing within the Kalihi-Ala Moana school impact district, with certain exceptions.  Effective 7/1/3000.  (SD1) The summary description of legislation appearing on this page is for informational purposes only and is not legislation or evidence of legislative intent.
