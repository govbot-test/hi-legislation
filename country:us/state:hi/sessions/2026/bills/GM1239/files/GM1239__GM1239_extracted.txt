A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO THE UNIVERSITY OF HAWAII.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that hands-on, real-world
2 experience is a critical component of higher education,
3 particularly in fields such as hospitality, agriculture,
4 culinary arts, and business management. To provide this
5 essential training, the university of Hawaii must be able to
6 operate teaching laboratories, which are facilities that
7 function as active businesses to give students practical
8 experience in their chosen careers.
9 The legislature further finds that these educational
10 laboratories often mimic commercial enterprises in their
11 operations, such as teaching hotels or culinary centers.
12 Consequently, these facilities can face regulatory hurdles at
13 the county level, where zoning codes and requirements for
14 special use permits, designed for commercial entities, are
15 applied to educational institutions. For example, the
16 university of Hawaii Maui college's hospitality academy seeks to
17 operate a limited number of guest rooms for student training but

1 faces zoning challenges regarding the collection of fees and the
2 requirement for special use permits.
3 The legislature determines that the educational mission of
4 the university of Hawaii is a matter of statewide concern
5 pursuant to article X, section 6, of the Hawaii State
6 Constitution. Subjecting university educational programs to
7 varying county zoning ordinances and discretionary permit
8 processes hinders the development of necessary workforce
9 training programs.
10 The purpose of this Act is to authorize the university of
11 Hawaii to engage in enterprise-related activities that have an
12 educational nexus on its campuses statewide, notwithstanding
13 county zoning laws or requirements for special use permits.
14 SECTION 2. Chapter 304A, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is
15 amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated
16 and to read as follows:
17 "§304A- Enterprise-related activities; educational
18 nexus; exemption front county regulation. (a) Notwithstanding
19 any law, county ordinance, or county zoning regulation to the
20 contrary, the university of Hawaii may engage in enterprise-

3 (5) Where applicable, general excise and transient

5 (e) For purposes of this section, "enterprise-related
6 activities" means structured, program-based operations conducted

1 (4) Are operated primarily for instructional, training, or
2 research purposes, and not as a primary commercial
3 enterprise; and
4 (5) May generate incidental revenue through the sale of
5 goods or services; provided that any such revenue is
6 secondary to and supportive of the educational
7 mission.
8 "Enterprise-related activities" may include but shall not be
9 limited to teaching laboratories, training facilities, clinics,
10 production environments, incubators, or other applied learning
11 settings designated by the board of regents that meet the
12 requirements of this section."
