C.D, 1
A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO CARE HOMES.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that many licensed care
2 homes in central and leeward Oahu operate in aging facilities
3 and serve vulnerable populations, including kupuna, individuals
4 with disabilities, and medically fragile residents. These
5 facilities are particularly susceptible to damage from natural
6 disasters, climate-related hazards, and prolonged power outages.
7 Strengthening the physical infrastructure and disaster
8 resilience of these care homes is critical to safeguarding
9 resident health and safety, minimizing emergency response needs,
10 and supporting community stability and recovery.
11 The purpose of this Act is to establish the central and
12 leeward Oahu care home community resilience pilot program to
13 assist eligible licensed care home facilities within the 96797,
14 96792, and 96786 postal zip codes on the island of Oahu in
15 enhancing their resilience against natural disasters, climate-
16 related risks, and extended utility disruptions.

1 SECTION 2. (a) The Hawaii emergency management agency
2 shall establish the central and leeward Oahu care home community
3 resilience pilot program.
4 (b) The purpose of the pilot program shall be to assist
5 eligible licensed care home facilities within the 96797, 96792,
6 and 96786 postal zip codes on the island of Oahu to implement
7 structural and operational resilience improvements; provided
8 that eligible structural and operational resilience improvements
9 may include but are not limited to;
10 (1) Roof reinforcement, hurricane clips, and
11 wind-resistant materials;
12 (2) Impact-resistant windows, storm shutters, and
13 strengthened doors;
14 (3) Backup power systems and battery storage for essential
15 medical equipment;
16 (4) Air filtration or cooling enhancements for extreme
17 heat or wildfire smoke;
18 (5) Fire-resistant landscaping and defensible space; and
19 (6) Shelter-in-place or safe room modifications.
20 (c) The pilot program may provide, as funds permit:

4 wildfire smoke, and power disruption risks;
5 (3) Technical assistance and training in preparedness and

7 (4) Support for emergency power and cooling solutions for

16 (5) Intermediate care facilities; and

19 96786 postal zip codes on the island of Oahu.

13 program pursuant to this Act.
14 The sum appropriated shall be expended by the Hawaii
15 emergency management agency for the purposes of this Act.
16 SECTION 4. This Act shall take effect on July 1, 2026, and
17 shall be repealed on June 30, 2029.
