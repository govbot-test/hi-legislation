Section 1:
SB2693 THE SENATE S.B. NO. 2693 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 STATE OF HAWAII A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO Capital improvement projects for AEROSPACE INFRASTRUCTURE. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that investment in aerospace and advanced aviation infrastructure is essential to the State's economic diversification and workforce development, as well as to national security objectives. The legislature further finds that Hawaii's geographic location, existing aviation infrastructure, and proximity to the United States Indo-Pacific command make the State uniquely positioned to support aerospace research, testing, and launch‑related activities. The legislature recognizes that public investment in supportive infrastructure at public airports, when paired with private capital investment, can generate long-term lease revenues, high-wage employment, and federal research and defense activities that benefit the State. The purpose of this Act is to appropriate moneys for the planning, design, and construction of an aerospace hangar and support infrastructure at Hilo international airport to support horizontal launch, advanced aviation, and aerospace operations, including defense-related users. SECTION 2. The director of finance is authorized to issue general obligation bonds in the sum of $15,000,000 or so much thereof as may be necessary and the same sum or so much thereof as may be necessary is appropriated for fiscal year 2026-2027 for the planning, design, and construction of an approximately fifty thousand square foot aerospace hangar and related support facilities at Hilo international airport; provided that no moneys from the appropriation shall be released unless matched dollar-for-dollar by the federal government, the private sector, or both, in accordance with section 4 of this Act. SECTION 3. The appropriation made for the capital improvement project authorized by this Act shall not lapse at the end of the fiscal biennium for which the appropriation is made; provided that all moneys from the appropriation unencumbered as of June 30, 2028, shall lapse as of that date. SECTION 4. The value of the matching contribution required by section 2 of this Act shall be documented and verified by the department of transportation prior to final acceptance of the project, and the match may be provided in phases concurrent with the State's expenditure of the moneys.  Matching contributions may include moneys from the private sector, the federal government, or both, equipment, or in-kind capital improvements; provided that no state general funds shall be used to satisfy the matching requirement. SECTION 5. The appropriation made under this Act shall be expended by the department of transportation for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 6. The department of transportation shall submit a report to the legislature no later than twenty days prior to the convening of the regular session of 2028 on the status of the project, including expenditures, matching contributions received, and anticipated economic and operational impacts. SECTION 7. This Act shall take effect on July 1, 2026.
