HCR160 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.C.R. NO. 160 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 STATE OF HAWAII HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION Urging all business and commercial establishments operating on Department of Hawaiian Home lands to display a portrait of The Honorable Prince Jonah Kūhiō Kalanianaole in a visible and prominent location. WHEREAS, every year on March 26 the state honors the life of Prince Jonah Kūhiō Kalanianaole and his many accomplishments in service of his people and the Hawaiian Islands; and WHEREAS, Prince Jonah Kūhiō Kalanianaole lived a remarkable life, born to High Chief David Kahalepouli Piikoi and Princess Kinoiki Kekaulike, the youngest daughter of Kaumualii, the last King of Kauai; and WHEREAS, at the young age of only thirteen years old, he was named a Prince by a royal proclamation from his Uncle, King Kalakaua; and WHEREAS, in 1902 Prince Jonah Kūhiō Kalanianaole was selected to serve as the Territory of Hawaii's non-voting delegate; and WHEREAS, some of Prince Kūhiō's most significant accomplishments include the establishment of Hawaii Volcanoes Nation Park, the appropriation of $27 million for the dredging and construction of Pearl Harbor, the establishment of Hawaiian civic clubs, the construction of a hospital at the Kaluapapa Settlement for lepers, and the sponsorship of the first bill for Hawaii's statehood in 1919; and WHEREAS, his most significant accomplishment was spearheading the passage of the Hawaiian Homes Commission Act in 1921 to provide homesteads for Native Hawaiians; and WHEREAS, the Hawaiian Homes Commission Act set aside 200,000 acres of land for Native Hawaiians as a form of rehabilitation after the annexation and seizure of land following the overthrow of the Hawaiian Kingdom in 1893; and WHEREAS, Prince Kūhiō's legacy lives on through the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands, which is responsible for 203,981 acres of trust lands, 10,045 homestead leases, and 47,068 lease applications by 29,451 Native Hawaiian beneficiaries; and WHEREAS, it is crucial that those operating on Department of Hawaiian Home Lands honor and memorialize the life and legacy of the honorable Prince Jonah Kūhiō Kalanianaole; now, therefore, BE IT RESOLVED by the House of Representatives of the Thirty-third Legislature of the State of Hawaii, Regular Session of 2026, the Senate concurring, all business and commercial establishments operating on Department of Hawaiian Home Lands are urged to display a portrait of the honorable Prince Jonah Kūhiō Kalanianaole in a visible and prominent location; and BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that certified copies of this Concurrent Resolution be transmitted to the Director of the Department of Hawaiian Home Lands and each member of the Hawaiian Homes Commission.
