Section 1:
HCR185 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.C.R. NO. 185 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 STATE OF HAWAII HOUSE CONCURRENT RESOLUTION urging all county planning and permitting agencies to deny all permit applications related to the construction, expansion, or use of warehouses by united states immigration and customs enforcement for the purpose of detaining individuals facing deportation. WHEREAS, in July 2025, Congress appropriated $45,000,000,000 to United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement to build new immigration detention centers; and WHEREAS, according to reports by the Washington Post, United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement intends to use these funds to acquire large warehouses to incarcerate people who are set to be deported; and WHEREAS, once operational, individuals will be transferred from local processing sites to the warehouses, some of which are expected to hold up to ten thousand people; and WHEREAS, once transferred to these warehouses, individuals could be far from their homes, families, and legal counsel; and WHEREAS, this system is dehumanizing, creates an immigration system rooted in mass incarceration, and deprives immigrants of individual dignity and due process; and WHEREAS, state and local officials across the country have expressed serious concerns regarding the health and safety of incarcerating individuals in these warehouses as well as the strain such facilities would place on local resources; and WHEREAS, the use of these warehouses for mass incarceration is contrary to Hawaii's priorities and values, which emphasize dignity and respect for all individuals, regardless of immigration status; now, therefore, BE IT RESOLVED by the House of Representatives of the Thirty-third Legislature of the State of Hawaii, Regular Session of 2026, the Senate concurring, that all county planning and permitting agencies are urged to deny all permit applications related to the construction, expansion, or use of warehouses by United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement for the purpose of detaining individuals facing deportation; and BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that certified copies of this Concurrent Resolution be transmitted to Director of the Department of Planning and Permitting of the City and County of Honolulu; Director of the Planning Department of the County of Maui; Planning Director of the Planning Department of the County of Hawaii; and Planning Director of the Planning Department of the County of Kauai. OFFERED BY: _____________________________ Report Title: United States Immigration and Customs Enforcement; Detention Warehouses; County Planning; Permits
