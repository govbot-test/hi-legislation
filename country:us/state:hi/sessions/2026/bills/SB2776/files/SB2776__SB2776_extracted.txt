Section 1:
SB2776 THE SENATE S.B. NO. 2776 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 STATE OF HAWAII A BILL FOR AN ACT Relating to Law enforcement. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  Chapter 139, Hawaii Revised Statutes, is amended by adding a new section to be appropriately designated and to read as follows: "§139-    State deputization; generalized policing.  (a)  A law enforcement officer may request assistance from a federal law enforcement officer in the case of a law enforcement emergency as defined in title 34 United States Code section 50102 or to prevent substantial risk of death or serious physical injury to another person. (b)  Notwithstanding subsection (a), a state employee or law enforcement officer shall not request assistance from a federal law enforcement officer to enforce state laws, including the following: (1)  Temporary or predetermined checkpoints or screenings; and (2)  Motor vehicle stops. (c)  Nothing in this section shall prevent federal law enforcement officers from making arrests without a warrant for a criminal offense under state law. (d) Any agreement between a law enforcement agency and federal agency for the purpose of criminal law enforcement shall be in writing, consistent with this section.  No agreement shall provide for federal participation in state checkpoints or screenings or motor vehicle stops.  No agreement shall exceed two years in duration and shall include training on state constitutional and statutory civil rights protections for federal law enforcement.  All agreements made pursuant to this subsection are subject to review by the department of the attorney general. (e) Nothing in this section shall be construed to prohibit a law enforcement agency or law enforcement officer from making a request for assistance from federal agencies in accordance with the requirements of this chapter." SECTION 2. New statutory material is underscored. SECTION 3. This Act shall take effect upon its approval.
