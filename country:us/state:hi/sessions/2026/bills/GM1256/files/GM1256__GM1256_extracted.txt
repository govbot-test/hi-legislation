A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO HABITUAL VIOLENT CRIME.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that Act 213, Session
2 Laws of Hawaii 2024 (Act 213), was enacted to deter certain
3 habitual violent crimes such as sexual assaults, abuse of family
4 or household members, and assaults against law enforcement
5 officers, by increasing the offense to a class C felony if the
6 person had three or more prior convictions for a violent crime
7 within a five-year period.
8 The legislature further finds that Act 213 sought to
9 protect the public from habitual violent criminal offenders but,
10 as a caution against the increased numbers of incarcerations,
11 also included a provision to repeal the offense of habitual
12 violent crime on June 30, 2027, pending an assessment of its
13 effectiveness. Given the amount of time it takes for felony
14 cases to travel through our criminal justice system, more time
15 is needed to evaluate Act 213's effectiveness in reducing
16 habitual violent crimes. For a valid assessment, best practices

3 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to:
4 (1) Require the attorney general, in consultation with the

6 annual reports to the legislature; and
7 (2) Amend Act 213 by:
8 (A) Requiring the criminal justice research institute
9 to submit a one-time report to the legislature
10 regarding the effectiveness of Act 213 in
11 reducing violent crime prior to the regular
12 session of 2031; and
13 (B) Extending its sunset date.
