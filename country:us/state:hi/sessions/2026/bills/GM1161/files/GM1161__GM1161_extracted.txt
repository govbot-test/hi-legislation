A BILL FOR AN ACT
RELATING TO TRANSPORTATION,
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII:
1 SECTION 1. The legislature finds that wing-in-ground
2 craft, which are often referred to as "seagliders", are an
3 emerging interisland mode of transportation. These vessels
4 utilize wing-in-ground-effect technology to provide a novel,
5 zero-emission, inexpensive, and highly efficient mode of
6 transportation. Wing-in-ground craft operate on water while in
7 harbors, leverage hydrofoils while transiting, and fly within a
8 wingspan's length above the water, enabling rapid transportation
9 between ports.
10 The legislature further finds that because wing-in-ground
11 craft operators aim to directly complement existing air travel
12 by providing statewide interisland transportation, subjecting
13 these operators to the public utilities commission's water
14 carrier regulations would impose undue oversight and increase
15 costs without improving public safety or consumer protection.
16 In particular, the legislature notes that the United States

8 (A) Section 225P-5, Hawaii Revised Statutes, which

14 state agency fleets; and

16 establishes a statewide goal of zero emissions
17 across all transportation modes, including ground
18 transportation and sea and air interisland
19 transportation.

3 Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to exempt persons
4 operating wing-in-ground craft engaged in the transportation of

11 cushion created by the aerodynamic lift caused by the ground
12 effect between the vessel and the water's surface."
