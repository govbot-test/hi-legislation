Section 1:
SB2877 HD1 THE SENATE S.B. NO. 2877 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 S.D. 1 STATE OF HAWAII H.D. 1 A BILL FOR AN ACT RELATING TO EDUCATION. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  (a)  The department of education shall establish a one-year student-authored book publication pilot program in which students research, write, and publish original works.  The pilot program shall be designed to strengthen literacy, analytical thinking, and communication skills; increase student engagement and confidence; support students' academic progression and preparation for postsecondary education; and foster connections to culture and community. (b) The student-authored book publication pilot program may be conducted at Kalihi public elementary schools and made available to students in grade five; provided that the pilot program shall be integrated into the existing grade five curricula of participating schools as a capstone. (c) Under the student-authored book publication pilot program: (1)  Each teacher may select a topic for their grade five class relating to Hawaiian culture or the local community; (2)  Each student shall write and illustrate a book on the selected topic; provided that the dimensions and length of the book shall be determined by the department of education according to available funds; and (3)  The department of education shall facilitate the collection, printing, binding, and return of the student-authored books. (d) The department of education shall contract with a third party pursuant to chapter 103D, Hawaii Revised Statutes, to implement the student-authored book publication pilot program. (e) The department of education shall submit a report on the student-authored book publication pilot program to the legislature no later than twenty days prior to the convening of the regular session of 2028.  The report shall include: (1)  Assessments of the pilot program's implementation, student outcomes, and the feasibility of continuing or expanding the pilot program; and (2)  Additional findings, recommendations, and any proposed legislation. SECTION 2. There is appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii the sum of $           or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 for the establishment of the student-authored book publication pilot program. The sum appropriated shall be expended by the department of education for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 3.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 3000. Report Title: DOE; Student-Authored Book Publication Pilot Program; Kalihi; Grade Five; Report; Appropriation Description: Requires the Department of Education to establish a one-year Student-Authored Book Publication Pilot Program.  Authorizes the Pilot Program to be conducted for fifth graders at Kalihi public elementary schools to be integrated into existing curricula.  Requires a report to the legislature.  Appropriates funds.  Effective 7/1/3000.  (HD1) The summary description of legislation appearing on this page is for informational purposes only and is not legislation or evidence of legislative intent.
