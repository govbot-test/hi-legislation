Section 1:
HB2057 HD1 HOUSE OF REPRESENTATIVES H.B. NO. 2057 THIRTY-THIRD LEGISLATURE, 2026 H.D. 1 STATE OF HAWAII A BILL FOR AN ACT MAKING AN APPROPRIATION TO THE DEPARTMENT OF INFORMATION TECHNOLOGY OF THE CITY AND COUNTY OF HONOLULU. BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF HAWAII: SECTION 1.  The legislature finds that Hawaii is unique among all other states in the nation for having driver's license and motor vehicle application systems managed and operated by a county within the state instead of having a state managed and operated application system.  This arrangement has its roots in the earliest days of statehood and the unique make-up of the four counties of the State. The legislature further finds that the application system currently being managed and operated by the city and county of Honolulu was developed in the 1970s and operates on a system of code that has become increasingly antiquated and that hinders further development and maintenance of this system due to its outdated technology.  Furthermore, the current application system is operated on a mainframe hosted by the city and county of Honolulu that itself is becoming increasingly outdated. The legislature finds that the upgrade of the existing application system will increase efficiency of operations, enable easier access to these systems by the departments of motor vehicles and law enforcement which access this information, and will reduce maintenance costs by switching from a physical mainframe maintained by the city and county of Honolulu to a cloud-based service. Accordingly, the purpose of this Act is to provide a grant‑in-aid to the department of information technology of the city and county of Honolulu for the upgrade of this vital system. SECTION 2.  There is appropriated out of the general revenues of the State of Hawaii the sum of $ or so much thereof as may be necessary for fiscal year 2026-2027 as a grant-in-aid to the department of information technology of the city and county of Honolulu for the implementation of upgrades to the driver's license and motor vehicle application systems; provided that no funds shall be expended unless the city and county of Honolulu provides matching funds on a dollar-for-dollar basis for the purpose for which this sum is appropriated. The sum appropriated shall be expended by the department of information technology of the city and county of Honolulu for the purposes of this Act. SECTION 3.  This Act shall take effect on July 1, 2026. Report Title: City and County of Honolulu Mayor's Package; Department of Information Technology; DMV systems; Grant-in-Aid; Appropriation Description: Appropriates moneys as a grant-in-aid to the City and County of Honolulu's Department of Information Technology to upgrade the driver's license and motor vehicle application systems, subject to a dollar-for-dollar match.  (HD1) The summary description of legislation appearing on this page is for informational purposes only and is not legislation or evidence of legislative intent.
